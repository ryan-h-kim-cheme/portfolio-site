## Overview
Project can be viewed from here: https://surfactant-ml-np6bcelkktfeggc6zpbhlv.streamlit.app/

Critical Micelle Concentration (CMC) is a crucial property of surfactants (amphiphalic molecules with hydrophilic head and hydrophobi tails) where major properties of the solution suddenly changes - mainly surface tension. While physical and thermodynamical models exists for such molecules, many times, these molecules tend to have properties difficult to predict a priori. Thus, the goal of this project was to utilize Machine Learning to help predict CMC, in particular the pCMC (-log(CMC)), of surfactants to aid in experimentation and practical usage of such surfactants. 

---

## Objectives

The major objectives were as follows:
- Explore various prediction and machine learning models to create more accurate predictions of critical micelle concentration (CMC)

---

## Results

XGBoost gave the most accurate results, with the highest R2 value and lowest error between the three models explored (Linear Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost)
