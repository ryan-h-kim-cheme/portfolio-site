## Overview

Participated in the CMU and Covestro "Rethink the Rink" Make-a-thon focused on redesigning hockey equipment to improve player safety, durability, and ergonomic performance through innovative engineering and advanced polymer materials. Collaborated with professional hockey players, industry engineers, and technical mentors to identify critical weaknesses in conventional hockey glove protection systems, particularly in high-impact hand and finger regions vulnerable to injury.

Developed a prototype hockey glove integrating advanced Covestro material solutions designed to improve impact absorption, structural durability, flexibility, and long-term wear resistance while maintaining player mobility and comfort. The project combined rapid engineering design, materials selection, finite element analysis (FEA), and prototype development within an accelerated 7-day engineering sprint environment.

The project incorporated:
- finite element analysis (FEA)
- impact mechanics
- polymer materials engineering
- CAD-based design iteration
- product prototyping
- durability optimization
- ergonomic engineering
- safety-focused mechanical design

to create an improved protective sports equipment concept for competitive hockey applications.

---

## Objectives

- Analyze key vulnerabilities in traditional hockey glove protection systems through collaboration with athletes and industry experts
- Improve hand and finger impact protection using advanced Covestro polymer materials and engineered padding systems
- Design a durable and lightweight hockey glove prototype optimized for both safety and player mobility
- Conduct finite element analysis (FEA) simulations to evaluate stress distribution, impact response, and structural performance
- Explore material-property relationships related to flexibility, toughness, energy absorption, and wear resistance
- Develop rapid engineering prototypes under accelerated competition timelines
- Present engineering calculations, design methodology, and simulation results to a professional panel of judges
- Apply engineering design principles to real-world sports safety and protective equipment challenges

---

## Results

- Successfully designed and developed a prototype hockey glove focused on enhanced hand protection and material durability
- Collaborated directly with professional hockey players and industry professionals to incorporate real-world performance feedback into the design process
- Conducted finite element analysis (FEA) simulations to evaluate impact loading conditions and optimize protective glove geometry
- Utilized Covestro material technologies to improve impact absorption, structural resilience, and long-term durability characteristics
- Delivered a final technical presentation including:
  - prototype development process
  - engineering calculations
  - FEA simulation results
  - materials selection rationale
  - product performance analysis
- Strengthened experience in:
  - rapid prototyping
  - engineering teamwork
  - technical communication
  - CAD and simulation workflows
  - safety-focused product engineering
  - materials-driven design optimization
